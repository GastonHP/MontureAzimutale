SkyWatcher 150P Goto Conversion by PheonixLolz on Thingiverse: https://www.thingiverse.com/thing:6808728

Summary:
I wanted my SkyWatcher to be goto capable. Currently it is in the ballpark of being on target but as the parts are 3d printed and I only ever did a one star alignment it is still off by a couple of arcmins. The goal of the project was to make viewing easier and that one is definitely accomplished. The setup uses 500:1 reduction pulley system for both axis and OnStepX on a Fysetc E4 board to drive the motors.For printing I suggest to lay each part flat so no supports are needed except for the AZ gear. That one has to be printed with the largest surface on the bed and supporting the bolt extrusion.Hardware used:For the ALT gear you can reuse the bolts that are already there and add one or two M10 nuts so the handle can be screwed in.AZ Gear needs a set of 5 M10*80 bolts and nuts and I added washers for support (I reused the washers from the existing bolt for the middle one).A 12" lazy suzan for the AZ movement and some ball bearings to smooth out the ALT movement (ball bearings are completely optional, the lazy suzan not however).A couple of wood screws to affix the motor mounts, lazy suzan and case to the scope (I used some small cheap local hardware store ones, but any will do as long as they are countersunk and small enough to not drill all the way through).For the ALT mount I also used a slide lock to quickly get the tension of the belt so I can balance the weight of the scope. You need one that fits the 7.5mm hole in the mount.For the hinge you will also need an set of M6*50 bolt and nut to attach the mount to the hinge.Stepper motors are NEMA17 200 steps motors bought from wherever you like. I got two discontinued Creality 3D CR-5 Pro 42-60-Y motors because they have some nice torque (if I remember correctly around 50 or 60 n/cm but I have seen larger scopes being moved around with smaller NEMA17's on the OnStep board).I attached a planetary gearbox with a 50:1 reduction to both stepper motors. I got mine from https://www.omc-stepperonline.com/ picking the one with the least arcminutes backlash.You also need two belts and 20 teeth pulleys to connect the motor to the printed gears (I bought an open ended belt and just cut and glued it to size, this took some trail and error to get the precise teeth count on the belts)And lastly the Fysetc E4 board (if you choose that one, look at the onstep wiki for some alternatives) some motor cables, a way to power the board (I used some fancy Alibaba toggle buttons and laptop power cable) but as long as the power supply is at least 12v 5A it should be fine whatever supply you get (please look at the Fysetc E4 tech specs for some options).!! A note for the AZ system, you have to drill a hole quite precisely in the center of it which is bigger than the AZ gear part that goes through it because the tension of the belt will pull the printed part against the drilled hole otherwise creating unwanted friction. I used a 9.4cm hole drill !!
